## Meta Requirements

### Thesis-Level Research Alignment Checklist

Academic research blogs translate rigorous research for broader audiences. Your writing should:

- [ ] **Accessible framing** — Lead with "why should anyone care?" not jargon
- [ ] **Research rigor visible** — Show methodology, not just conclusions
- [ ] **Contribution clarity** — What's novel vs. what's background
- [ ] **Honest uncertainty** — What you know, what you hypothesize, what you don't know
- [ ] **Literature grounding** — Position within existing research landscape
- [ ] **Future-oriented** — Where this leads, open questions, research agenda

**Academic Blog Voice Examples:**
- ✅ "My thesis explores [question]. Here's the intuition behind why this matters..."
- ✅ "Prior work showed X. I'm investigating whether Y holds under conditions Z..."
- ✅ "These results are preliminary. Here's what I can and cannot conclude..."
- ❌ "I solved [grand challenge]" (overclaiming)
- ❌ "[Wall of jargon without context]" (inaccessible)
- ❌ "Obviously, [contested claim]" (hiding uncertainty)

---

### Academic Integrity Requirements

- [ ] Advisor acknowledgment (if appropriate)
- [ ] Funding sources disclosed
- [ ] Collaborator contributions noted
- [ ] Pre-publication considerations addressed
- [ ] No scooping of unpublished results

---

### Audience Calibration

Your blog serves multiple audiences:
- **General technical readers**: Need intuition and motivation
- **Adjacent researchers**: Need positioning and methodology
- **Domain experts**: Need technical depth and novelty
- **Future employers**: Need to see your thinking process

Structure to serve all: accessible intro → rigorous middle → implications end.

### The Hook (100-150 words)

- Start with something surprising, important, or counter-intuitive
- Why should a smart non-expert care?
- Preview what you'll cover

**Example hooks:**
- "What if computers didn't use binary?"
- "The human brain uses 20 watts. The best AI chips use 300. What's the gap?"
- "Most [field] assumes [X]. My research questions that assumption."

## Writing Notes

### Thesis Blog Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with motivation | Lead with jargon |
| Show your thinking process | Just report results |
| Acknowledge uncertainty | Overclaim |
| Credit collaborators | Take sole credit |
| Explain for multiple audiences | Write only for experts |
| Share failures and pivots | Hide the messy parts |

### Balancing Rigor and Accessibility

- Technical depth IS compatible with clarity
- Every equation should have an intuition
- Every jargon term should have a definition
- Experts won't mind explanations; non-experts need them

### Pre-Publication Considerations

- Consult advisor before posting unpublished results
- Consider scooping risk for competitive areas
- "I'm working on X" can be safer than "I found X"
- Framing as "research in progress" preserves publication options

### Building Your Research Identity

- Consistent voice across posts
- Show evolution of thinking
- Connect posts to each other
- Build toward thesis narrative
